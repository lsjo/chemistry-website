Gallium Arsenide

NameGallium Arsenide
Chemical FormulaGaAs
AppearanceGray Crystals
ConductivitySemi-conductor
Bonding TypeMixed Character

Gallium Arsenide is a semi-conductor with many different applications. It has many applications and uses, including in microwave frequency integrated circuits, infrared LEDs, laser diodes, solar cells, computing, and can even be used to grow other semi-conductors. Gallium Arsenide is important because of its bonding type - described as ‘mixed character’, Gallium Arsenide is neither covalent or ionic. This is because they have a medium level of electronegativity, and form semi-polarised molecules. These unique bonds can be described with several different bonding models. Due to its properties, Gallium Arsenide also has several positive and negative environmental impacts that are worth considering.
